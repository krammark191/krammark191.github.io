The Space Cloud Site Plan

Overview

Purpose

Hi! My name is Mark. I want to build a website that shows you a different picture of space every time you visit it. Each picture would be of any medium and have information about the photographer or artist, the picture itself, as well as information on how to acquire the picture. This would be a creative way to showcase a wide variety of space-related art and provide the artists another platform to display their art. Would you like to be a part of this?

Audience

My intended audience is anyone who likes pictures of space! Space is really cool and I want to be able to share it with anyone. Anyone who wants to look at various works of art will find that my website will have just what they're looking for. It will also have a section for those who want to purchase art.

Content

Home page

Information

The Space Cloud is intended to share amazing media about universe with anyone who wishes to see it. The site will start small, with just basic functionality, with the core focus being a new picture, video, or piece of artwork displayed every time the site is visited. Having a wide variety of space media is a fun way to share images and videos of our vast surroundings with those stuck in their daily lives. I intend to have information about each piece of media included below, where it is out of the way until it is needed, leaving the screen space to the media only. There could be small questionnaires on the site to gather more information, there could be artwork competitions, etc. There is a lot of space out in space, and I’d like to reflect that on the website. Information needed to complete any of the available activities will be available on the site itself.

History

My goal is to make this website unique and fun. I would like to maybe have an exploration page where it starts with an image of our solar system, and you click on different planets to learn more about them. It would be very cool to have loads of information and sources available for anyone to discover. I love space and there is so much to learn about it, and I want to be able to share as much information with people as I can. Something that could be very cool is to have sections for different aspects of space, such as the physics part of space, especially near black holes. Other topics could include constellations, space travel, planets, etc. The possibilities are endless. With so much space, there are so many things one can do.

Images for the Home page

Hall of Fame

It would be neat to have a sort of hall of fame for famous pictures or videos or audio recordings from space, such as the first image of a black hole, the Apollo 11 moon landing recording, etc. Having a page dedicated to the history of space travel and innovation, having a page dedicated to the current endeavors in space exploration, and having a page dedicated to the future of space flight. There has been a resurgence of space exploration in the recent years, the likes of which haven’t been seen since the late sixties. It’s an exciting time for us, we’re at one of the most interesting times in human history. There is so much happening in so many places. While we sit here there are two billionaires battling it out in the space exploration industry. Who knew that the future of space travel would be in the hands of two billionaires locked in a modern-day space race?

Images for the Page 2

James Webb Space Telescope

In the midst of all this excitement, the James Webb Space Telescope is being launched into orbit this year! Just pictures and videos of this thing being built are incredible in their own right, it’ll be exciting to see what it’s able to produce when it’s up in space. The James Webb Space Telescope is the biggest space telescope ever built and will dwarf the Hubble Space Telescope in its capabilities. This new behemoth of modern space technology has been in the works for over fifteen years. The time has finally arrived when we get to see the benefits of this amazing new work of technological art. This new creation will allow us to see even more of space in even more detail than we’ve ever seen it before. The manufacturing process of this telescope is a feat of engineering on its own. We live in an exciting time.
